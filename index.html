Life Story

Kathleen's Life Story

Kathleen

Kathleen's life story began when she was born December 13, 1960, in Chicago, Illinois, to Gertrude Scott  and Marshall Thompson.

Growing up in Chicago, Illinois, Kathleen and her siblings (Catherine , Peggy Ann , Charles C. Scott , Mary Jane, Larry James , Gregory , Anthony , Laura Ann , and Carolyn), were mostly raised by oldest sister Catherine, with help from sister Peggy Ann, while mother Gertrude worked to generate income.

Kathleen met the love of her life, Bernard Wynn, who lived in a nearby neighborhood not far from where the Scott family lived.

On August 3, 1975, Kathleen and Bernard would welcome their first of two children together, a baby girl, Shalena Scott.

A few years later, on April 28, 1978, Kathleen and Bernard would welcome their second child into the world, a baby girl, Angel Catherine Scott.

Kathleen's family now totaled two children, and she would go on to become a grandmother and mother-in-law, as well.

"Big Kat"

Gone, But Not Forgotten . . .

Scott

Kathleen earned her High School Diploma and took classes in Accounting and Business Administration.

She worked as a TSA Officer for 19 years prior to her passing.  She enjoyed her job and received many accolades from co-workers and passengers.  Her gentle nature prompted her to be of service to many and she was loved by all.

Although Kathleen was not a person of big physical stature, she was big on everything!  Big on enjoying life and having fun, big on meeting and talking to people, and big on helping others.  "Big Kat" is what they called her.

So jovial and friendly, she could sit down and talk to anyone about anything.  She and her daughters, Shalena and Angel were inseparable!  They were like three peas in a pod.  Always together!  Her children, family, and multitude of friends loved her so much!  Why?  Because "Big Kat" was soooo much fun to be around!

Called to Glory

Kathleen Called to Glory

Kathleen accepted Christ at an early age.  Her mother often took the family to Bible class and Sunday service.  She was a member of Christ Deliverance Church.  Her faith in the Lord was persistent and she often called upon Him.

 Kathleen gained her wings on August 11, 2022.  At the time of her passing, she was years old.  She was preceded in death by her mother, Gertrude Scott, her dad, Marshall Thompson, and her brothers Charles C. Scott and Larry James Scott.

Kathleen leaves behind her two daughters Shalena Scott-Holmes (Courtney Holmes) and Angel Scott-Thomas (Jimmy Thomas).  Her special friend, Mikhail Gololobov, two brothers Gregory Scott (Christine Scott), Anthony Scott (Pearl Scott), five sisters Catherine Scott, Peggy Ann Lyons, Mary Jane Scott-Lawrence, Laura Ann Scott, Carolyn Marie Scott, three grandsons Chrishaun Scott, Aspen Scott, Jax Thomas and one great-granddaughter Chrissy Sade Scott, and old childhood friend, Shawn Lundy and BBF Versa Lee Frazier.

Additionally, she leaves a host of nieces, nephews, cousins, special daughters and sons and friends.

Kathleen's Poems

"I Am Free . . ."

Don't grieve for me, for now I'm free, I'm following the path God laid for me.  I took His hand when I heard His call, I turned my back and left it all.

I could not stay another day, To laugh, to love, to work, to play.  Tasks left undone must stay that way.  I've found that peace at the close of the day.

If my parting has left a void, Then fill it with remembered joy.  A friendship shared, a laugh, a kiss, Ah yes, these things I too will miss.

Be not burdened with times of sorry, I wish you the sunshine of tomorrow.  My life's been full, I savoured much, Good friends, good times, a loved one's touch,

Perhaps my time seemed all to brief, Don't lengthen it now with undue grief.  Lift up your heart and share with me, God wanted me now, He set me free.

Author:  Shannon Lee Moseley

Contributed By:  Anonymous

"Footprints In The Sand . . ."

One night I had a dream.  As I was walking along the beach with my Lord.  Accross the dark sky flashed scenes from my life.  For each scene, I notice two sets of footprints in the sand.  One belonging to me and the other to my Lord.

After the last scene of my life flashed before me, I looked back at the footprints in the sand.  I noticed that at many times along the path of my life, especially at the very lowest and saddest times, there was only one set of footprints.

This really troubled me, so I asked the Lord about it.  "Lord you said once I decided to follow you, You'd walk with me all the way.  But I noticed that during the saddest and most troublesome times of my life, there was only one set of footprints.  I don't understand why, when I needed You the most, You would leave me."

He whispered, "My precious child, I love you and will never leave you.  Never, ever during your trials and testings.   When you saw only one set of footprints, it was then that I carried you".

Kathleen's Tributes

Gregory

My sister Kathleen never really called me by my name.  She mostly just called me "brother".  She would simply say, hey brother "this" or hey brother "that".  She was a real dancer, I mean she had moves, she could flat out do some steppin.  I believe she was good enough to compete and win on "Dancing With The Stars".  It was amazing to watch her and her dance partner effortlessly glide accross the floor.

I really couldn't dance a lick, but she would always pull me onto the dance floor with her.  She would say, "brother", I'm going to spin around, and without looking behind me, fall backwards, and you better be there to catch me!

I never once let her hit the floor.  She was sooo carefree and I loved her sooo much!  She was more than a sister to my wife Christine and me.  She was a friend who was always available when we needed her.

Like my mother and brothers who passed away before her, she too, will live in my heart.
